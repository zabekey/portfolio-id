My Role:

As a individual project, I chose to observe user behavior with a interface, conduct interviews, and summarized observaions into personas and storyboards. This project assited in helping me learn the human rooted nature that interfaces must be attentive to in order to accurately serve their purpose and serve users.

The Process and Results:

This process was broken into three parts:

1: Choose an interface to observe user interactions and conduct interviews

2: Create personas based on these observations and interviews

2: Utilized one of my personas in order to create a user stroyboard interaction

Part 1: Interface Observation and Interviews

The public interface I chose to observe was the water dispenser in the kitchen on the 4th floor of the CIT at Brown University. This dispenser is infamous within the CIT and to those who frequently visit the building and a sketch of it can be found below. I then observed users natually interacting with this interface and interveiwed them asking various questions regrading theri experience.

  • Dark and gloomy, plaing into the office/work feel of the interior of the building
  • Colors often incosistent throughout the builing
  • Very little synergy and complement between the colors
  • Lack of natural lighting to make the space warmer and brighter

Part 2.1: CIT Color Palette Redesign

After exploring the CIT and analyzing the current state of the interior I came up with the color palette seen below in order to address the issues above.

Notable improvements:

  • Neutral and consistent colors that warm up the space indicate the space is meant for productivity
  • colors contrast well with each other accessibilty purposes
  • Utilized colors that are not assocaited with any type of color blindness
  • Colors complement the current furniture colors (mainly brown and gray) within the CIT
