Paint the CIT

The Objective:

The Department of Computer Science (CIT) at Brown University is a building near and dear to all computer science majors here and an interface that student interact with nearly daily. I was made aware that the building was undergoing a renovation when it came to the colors within the building in order to offer a potential solution to thier rennovation alongside other designers submissions

As an avid CIT admirer as well as passionate designer I thought, thought I would take a shot a redesigning the colors within the CIT myself and creating color palette for the redesign to follow, which was the objective of this project.

My Role:

I served as interior designer for when it came to the colors used within the CIT for things like the walls, furniture, railings, and more. Specifically, I was charged with choosing colors that satisfy accessiblity, contrast, the enviroment's purpose, and color rlies to rennovate the building effectivly and have colors that work for the forseeable future.

The Process and Results:

This design process was broken into two parts:

1: Explore and understand the current color palette of the CIT when it came to its pros and cons, functionality indication, and accessibilty.

2: Create my own color palette for the interior to flilow as well as create sketches of what the interior of the building would look like using this color palette compared to its current state.

Part 1: Exploring the CIT

In order to understand how to create a valid color palette redesign for the CIT, I had to first understand the current issues within the interior of the CIT. My observations are compilied in a list below.

  • Dark and gloomy, plaing into the office/work feel of the interior of the building
  • Colors often incosistent throughout the builing
  • Very little synergy and complement between the colors
  • Lack of natural lighting to make the space warmer and brighter

Part 2.1: CIT Color Palette Redesign

After exploring the CIT and analyzing the current state of the interior I came up with the color palette seen below in order to address the issues above.

Conclusion:

Although my design was not chosen for the rennovation of the CIT I was still able to take a lot away from going through the process of this project.

Firstly, I was able to understand hwo much of an impact that color plays within different aspects of design from accessibilty to the functionality of the space. I was also able to understand how to think critically about the pros and cons of the current design of an inteface and reflect in order to suggest improvements. Lastly, I was abel to experince going through the back and forth process of chosing differnt colors and designs until I landed on one that I was content with an thought would serve the purpose the best.

Overall I was able to go through a redesign process in which I learned more about this process and the impact of colors on a interface.
